# Light Years per Day (ly/day)

**Light Years per Day** (abbreviated as **ly/day**) is a unit of velocity that measures how many light years an object travels in one day. It combines the concept of *distance*‚Äîmeasured in **light years**‚Äîwith *time*, specifically days, to express the speed at which something moves across vast cosmic distances.

**Relationship to other units:**

- A **light year** (~9.461 √ó 10^12 km or ~5.878 √ó 10^12 miles) is the distance light travels in one year.
- Since a year has approximately **365.25 days**, light travels about **1 light year per year**.
- To convert **light years per day** to more familiar velocity units:
  
  \[
  1\, \text{ly/day} = \frac{1\, \text{light year}}{1\, \text{day}} \approx \frac{9.461 \times 10^{12}\, \text{km}}{1\, \text{day}}
  \]
  
  which is approximately **1.095 √ó 10^8 km/sec** (about 36.5% the speed of light).

**Key point:**  
**Light Years per Day is an exceptionally high velocity, used mainly in theoretical or astrophysical contexts to describe extremely rapid cosmic motions.**
